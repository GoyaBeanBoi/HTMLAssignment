The Life and Music of Ben Mini

Ben Mini is a musician and audio engineer based in Miami, Florida. He plays instruments such as bass, guitar, and piano, and produces and engineers music.

About Ben Mini

Early Life and Background

Ben grew up in a small suburban town called Reading, Massachusetts. He got into music because of his parents, who signed him up for guitar lessons when he was 5 years old. He quit a year later, then returned to it at age 8. Early influences included blues and 90s rock, especially SRV and the Red Hot Chili Peppers.

Career and Current Work

Ben studies at the Frost School of Music in Music Engineering. His focus areas are bass, live sound, and record production. He performs frequently around Miami on upright and electric bass and is currently mixing and mastering projects for artists across the country.

Achievements and Collaborations

  1. Performed at Clearwater Jazz Festival, Carnegie Hall, and The Middle East (Boston).
  2. Works for JAS Academy in Aspen, Colorado with Christian McBride; has worked with Etienne Charles, Lisa Fisher, José James, Brian Lynch, René Marie, Monty Alexander, and Benny Green.
  3. Ran live sound at Frost for Marcus Miller, Jacob Collier, and Pat Metheny; recipient of a DownBeat award for Studio Record Production.

Musical Interests and Fun Facts

  • Big influences include Christian McBride; loves funk artists like Stevie Wonder and Hiatus Kaiyote.
  • Played French horn and trumpet for marching band.
  • Played hockey through high school as a defenseman.
